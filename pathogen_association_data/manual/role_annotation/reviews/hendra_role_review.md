# Hendra Role Review

## Scope

- Disease tracker label: `Hendra`
- Source pathogen: `Henipavirus hendraense`
- Modelling frame: non-vectored, host SDM needed, reservoir hosts primary; horse SDM can be an exposure layer.

## Source-Backed Host Roles

- `Pteropus alecto` (`9402`): reservoir host. CDC identifies Pteropus flying foxes as carrying Hendra virus in nature; Scientific Reports treats `P. alecto` as a likely primary natural reservoir; 2026 EID reports natural infections in captive Australian black flying foxes.
- `Pteropus conspicillatus` (`328804`): reservoir host, review-visible. Scientific Reports treats this species with `P. alecto` as the likely reservoir pair; CFSPH notes stronger infection/shedding evidence than for `P. poliocephalus` or `P. scapulatus`.
- `Pteropus poliocephalus` (`9403`): genotype-qualified reservoir host, review-visible. Peel et al. 2022 detected HeV-g2 in urine from grey-headed flying foxes and concluded `P. alecto` and `P. poliocephalus` excrete HeV-g2 in urine and are likely competent reservoir hosts. Caveat: HeV-g2 urine excretion / likely competent reservoir support; not primary HeV-g1 reservoir.
- `Pteropus scapulatus` (`94117`): incidental/susceptible Australian flying fox host, review-visible. It remains out of the reservoir bucket here because the checked HeV-g2 urine-excretion source did not screen `P. scapulatus` urine, and current direct evidence does not support a reservoir/shedding role.
- `Equus caballus` (`9796`): amplifying/spillover exposure host. CDC and Queensland Health describe flying fox-to-horse exposure and horse-to-horse / horse-to-human transmission.
- `Homo sapiens` (`9606`): dead-end/incidental spillover host. CDC and CFSPH describe human cases after contact with infected horses and no documented person-to-person or direct flying fox-to-human transmission.

## Proxy Decision

No broad Pteropodidae or Chiroptera proxy rule was added. The current roster contains several serology-only or non-Australian pteropodid rows, and applying a broad reservoir proxy would overstate modelling roles. Exact Australian flying fox assignments should drive the role bucket.
