- Broad genus focused review with strong focus on Paradoxopsyllus.
- I kept both the experimentally demonstrated vector rows and the field infection only rows because the review explicitly treats them as plague-relevant.
- `Paradoxopsyllus dashidorzhii`, `Paradoxopsyllus scorodumovi`, `Paradoxopsyllus repandus`, and `Paradoxopsyllus teretifrons` are the most mixed rows because the paper shows context dependent performance or inconsistent transmission.
- `Paradoxopsyllus curvispinus`, `Paradoxopsyllus custodis`, `Paradoxopsyllus hesperius kalabukhovi`, `Paradoxopsyllus integer`, and `Paradoxopsyllus scalonae` are uncertainty rows based on infection or role claims rather than direct competence experiments.

Extraction notes

- Broad regional review of Brazilian plague foci.
- I kept the naturally infected taxa that the review explicitly names and the taxa where lab transmission is described.
- `Polygenis tripus` is mixed because the paper supports efficient vector status in one focus but says its role in Serra dos Orgãos is unknown.
- `Polygenis bohlsi jordani` and `Xenopsylla cheopis` are the strongest rows in this source.
- `Pulex irritans` and `Ctenocephalides felis` are field-detection rows only in this paper.
