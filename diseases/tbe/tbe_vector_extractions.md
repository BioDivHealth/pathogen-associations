## Belova et al. - 2023 - Hybrids of Ixodes ricinus and Ixodes persulcatus ticks effectively acquire and transmit tick-borne e.pdf

```csv
tick-borne encephalitis,Ixodes ricinus,tick,confirmed,lab,,Belova et al. 2023,main vector; lab competence
tick-borne encephalitis,Ixodes persulcatus,tick,confirmed,lab,,Belova et al. 2023,main vector; lab competence
```

Extraction notes

- Narrow lab study; not a complete species list.
- The paper explicitly treats `Ixodes ricinus` and `Ixodes persulcatus` as the main vectors and tests them experimentally.
- Hybrid ticks were not extracted because the schema is species-based.

```csv
tick-borne encephalitis,Dermacentor reticulatus,tick,candidate,field,Poland,Biernat et al. 2014,PCR only
```

Extraction notes

- Narrow single-species field study from Poland; partial support only, not a complete vector list.
- The row is kept conservative because the paper reports TBEV RNA in natural ticks rather than transmission competence.

## Chitimia-Dobler et al. - 2019 - Repeated isolation of tick-borne encephalitis virus from adult Dermacentor reticulatus ticks in an e.pdf

```csv
tick-borne encephalitis,Dermacentor reticulatus,tick,probable,field+lab,northern Saxony Germany,Chitimia-Dobler et al. 2019,adult pools; repeated isolation; equal role
tick-borne encephalitis,Ixodes ricinus,tick,confirmed,field+lab,northern Saxony Germany,Chitimia-Dobler et al. 2019,nymph pools only; common vector in source
```

Extraction notes

- Narrow primary study; not a complete species list.
- `Dermacentor reticulatus` is kept at `probable` because the paper supports it with repeated field detection and isolation and then argues for an equal circulation role.
- `Ixodes ricinus` is supported by positive nymph pools and the source's explicit background framing as the common TBE vector.

## Mittova et al. - 2024 - Tick-Borne Encephalitis Virus (TBEV) Epidemiology, Diagnosis, Therapeutic Approaches and Some Molec.pdf

```csv
tick-borne encephalitis,Ixodes ricinus,tick,confirmed,review,Europe,Mittova et al. 2024,principal vector in Europe

tick-borne encephalitis,Haemaphysalis concinna,tick,probable,review,Asia,Mittova et al. 2024,major vector in Asia
```

Extraction notes

- Broad review; partial support only, not a complete species list.
- I kept the named species-level vector claims and did not expand genus-only mentions of `Dermacentor`, `Hyalomma`, or `Rhipicephalus`.
- `Haemaphysalis concinna` is the more uncertain row because it is framed as a major vector in Asia within a review summary.
